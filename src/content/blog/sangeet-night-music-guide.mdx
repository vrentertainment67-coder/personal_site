## Ambient Arrival

Guests are walking in, finding seats, greeting family. The DJ is not the star yet. Music should feel warm, celebratory, and completely undemanding. This is background — intentional background.

<CalloutBox variant="insight" label="DJ VIC'S TAKE">
The goal here is subliminal. Nobody should feel like the DJ is performing yet. They should just feel good walking in.
</CalloutBox>

## Performance Support Set

<BlogImage

This is the heart of the sangeet. Families and friends have rehearsed for weeks. The DJ's job is purely support — cue tracks on time, manage transitions cleanly, hold steady energy between groups.

<CalloutBox variant="tip" label="DJ VIC'S TAKE">
Get every performing group's track list at least one week before. Know your cue points cold. A bad cue on a family performance is unforgivable — the room never forgets it.
</CalloutBox>

## DJ Opens — High Energy

Performances wrap. The room is warm, loose, emotional. This is the moment the DJ steps forward and takes ownership. You do not open soft here — you open high and don't look back.

<CalloutBox variant="insight" label="DJ VIC'S TAKE">
There's a natural beat of applause when performances end — that's your entry point. I drop something that makes the floor erupt immediately. No fade-in. No build-up. Just go.

<SectionDivider label="SET 06 — FINALE" />

## The Closing Finale

A sangeet should not wind down. It should peak and then stop. Pull the crowd to their highest energy point — then close before the room has a chance to dip.

<CalloutBox variant="warning" label="THE RULE">
End decisively. A great finale leaves the room wanting one more song. That feeling is the review that gets you referred.
</CalloutBox>

<div style="background:#111111; border:1px solid #1E1E1E; padding:8px 20px; border-radius:2px;">
  <FAQItem question="How many songs should a sangeet playlist have?">
    A full sangeet runs 4–6 hours — roughly 80–120 songs. But the real playlist is written by the crowd on the night. A good DJ has 3× as many tracks prepped as they'll actually play.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="Should I give the DJ a song list or let them decide?">
    Both. Give 10–15 non-negotiables, then step back. The worst sangeet playlists come from couples who hand over 200 songs and expect every track to play. A curated short list plus a trusted DJ produces a far better night.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="What's the difference between a sangeet and a reception playlist?">
    Sangeet is about performance, choreography, and communal celebration — the playlist actively pulls people onto the floor. Reception balances dinner ambience, couple moments, and open-floor energy. They are genuinely different emotional jobs.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="Can a DJ play both Bollywood and Western music at a sangeet?">
    Absolutely. In 2026, the best sangeet sets blend both intelligently. The key is sequencing — bring Western and crossover tracks into the modern and finale sets after the classics have already unified the room. In Bangalore, a 70/30 Bollywood-to-Western split in the later sets works consistently well.
  </FAQItem>
</div>
