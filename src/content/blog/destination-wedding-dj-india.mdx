## The Logistics Are the Job

At a Bangalore wedding, a DJ drives their gear to the venue. At a destination wedding, equipment travels by flight, road, or freight — and every kilometre of that journey is a risk point.

<CalloutBox variant="insight" label="DJ VIC'S TAKE">
I travel with a core kit that fits airline-compliant cases. For large destination weddings, I arrange freight logistics for the full rig. Both scenarios need planning 4–6 weeks in advance minimum.
</CalloutBox>

<div style="background:#111111; border:1px solid #1E1E1E; padding:8px 20px 4px; border-radius:2px; margin:20px 0;">

  <ChecklistItem>Who is responsible if equipment is damaged in transit?</ChecklistItem>

  <ChecklistItem>Do they have experience with the specific venue or venue type?</ChecklistItem>

<SectionDivider label="02 — OUTDOOR ACOUSTICS" />

## Outdoor Venues Change Everything

<BlogImage
  src="/images/blog/destination-wedding-venue.jpg"
  alt="Outdoor destination wedding venue with stage"
  caption="Sound behaves entirely differently in an open field, a heritage courtyard, or on a beach. Experience with outdoor acoustics is non-negotiable."
/>

A ballroom is predictable. An outdoor venue in Udaipur or a beach setup in Goa is not. Sound behaves entirely differently without walls — dispersion, wind interference, and neighbour noise restrictions all require a DJ who's handled these environments before.

<CalloutBox variant="warning" label="CHECK THIS">
Many destination venues have noise curfews — typically 10pm or 11pm for outdoor music. Confirm the exact decibel and time limits before your set planning. A DJ who doesn't ask about this hasn't done enough destination work.
</CalloutBox>

<SectionDivider label="03 — INTERNATIONAL GUESTS" />

## Mixed International Guest Lists

Destination weddings often have a higher percentage of NRI and international guests. The musical brief changes — there's more room for Western and global tracks, but you still need to honour the Indian families in the room.

<CalloutBox variant="insight" label="DJ VIC'S TAKE">
International guest lists are actually my favourite brief. I've played for crowds from 12 nationalities at a single event. The set architecture is more layered — but the floor can go places a standard Bollywood wedding never reaches.
</CalloutBox>
